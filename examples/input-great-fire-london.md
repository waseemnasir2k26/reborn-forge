## Notes

- Era cap: late-Stuart London, 17th-c timber-frame Tudor townhouses, narrow medieval street plan (the fire's cause), wooden water-pipes, leather fire-buckets, no steam-powered fire engines (post-1721).
- Anachronism trap: do NOT show plague-doctor beak masks (Great Plague of 1665 was the *previous* year — beak masks ARE period-correct here, 1619 onward, but most Londoners did not wear them — keep them rare and only on physicians).
- Cultural beat: Pepys burying his Parmesan cheese + papers in his garden is a signature scene; Pepys's diary (Sept 2–7 1666) is the canonical primary source.
- Visual motif: pick "burning city / smoke" — this is the literal genre-archetype.
- POV: Scholar-omniscient with Pepys-diary frame device, OR Trade-route-map narrator showing fire spread block by block.

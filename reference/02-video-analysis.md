**INPUT:**

## [FIRST FRAME]

Extreme close-up on a grim object — a hooded body / a hand on a bell-rope / a single torch in a stone passage — rendered in deep shadow with one hard key light. Skin tones pushed yellow-green (plague pallor signature). 80% of frame is in shadow. No on-screen text yet.

## [SCROLL STOP TRIGGER]

**Visceral physical detail + cinematic-grade rendering at 9:16 or 16:9.** Viewer's scroll-pattern recognizes "this is not a stock-image history channel" within the first 0.4 seconds. The chiaroscuro + film-grain + halation reads as cinema, not slideshow. Pattern-interrupt secondary trigger: the absence of music for the first 8 sec — silence + a single church bell at ~3.0 sec is unusual on YouTube and registers as deliberate.

## [VISUAL OR NARRATIVE CONTRADICTION]

The contradiction is **calm narration over horrific image.** Voice is near-whisper, almost mournful, deliberately slow (~135 wpm). Image is rendered horror — bodies, smoke, flies. The dissonance — quiet voice + violent visual — is what holds attention longer than either alone.

Secondary contradiction: **modern AI-generated photoreal cinema applied to medieval subject matter.** Viewer expects engravings or CGI; gets photoreal. Surprise = retention.

## [SECTION FLOW]

**S1 — COLD DREAD (0:00–0:08).** Single grim image. One-line stake spoken in present tense. Silence + single bell. No music.

**S2 — ORIGIN (0:08–2:30, ~25%).** Date + place anchor ("It was the year 1347…"). Patient zero or causal vector (rats, fleas, ship from Caffa). Trade-route map shot. Music enters at −22 LUFS, dark-ambient drone in D minor.

**S3 — SPREAD (2:30–6:00, ~50%).** City after city. Mortality numbers climb on screen. Map zoom-out → pull-back → zoom-in pattern. Hoofbeats, cart wheels, distant tolling bells.

**S4 — COLLAPSE (6:00–9:00, ~75%).** Bodies. Mass graves. Abandoned villages. Iconic imagery — flagellants, plague-pit, abandoned market. Music crescendos to its highest sustained level (still −18 LUFS, never above VO).

**S5 — AFTERMATH (9:00–11:00, ~92%).** Society broken + remade. Labor shortage. Religious upheaval. Slight warm color reintroduction. Music falls to a drone.

**S6 — ECHO + CTA (11:00–12:00, ~100%).** "The world that emerged was not the same." Single bell. Silence. Soft single-line CTA.

## [PACING]

- **Average shot duration:** 4.5 seconds across the longform (range 3–8s).
- **Hero hold time:** 8–12 seconds on key tableaux (mass grave, abandoned city, ship arrival).
- **Cold-open shot:** 8 seconds, single image, single Ken Burns push-in.
- **Total runtime:** ~12 minutes (typical for the channel).
- **Scene count:** ~42 distinct rendered scenes.
- **Transitions:** 95% straight cuts with 0.3–0.5s soft cross-dissolve. No fancy wipes.

## [TRANSFORMATION TYPE]

**Tension → Payoff,** but layered:

- Macro tension (will the plague reach London?) → Macro payoff (it does, half of London is dead by Nov 1348).
- Micro tension per stage (will the doctors find a cure? will the flagellants stop the spread?) → Micro payoff (they don't; nothing works).
- Inverted-arc tension at end: the *survival* of society is the payoff, but it's a survival into a world that's not the same.

There is no "before → after" beauty arc. The transformation is **before → worse → quiet endurance.**

## [SCRIPT STRUCTURE]

Narrated, third-person, past-tense, literary-grim. Sentence length leans long (15–25 words avg). Frequent use of:

- Date + place anchors as paragraph openers ("In the autumn of 1347, a Genoese galley sailed into Messina harbor…")
- Named-individual zoom ("A scholar named Gabriele de' Mussi recorded what happened next…")
- Mortality stats with sources ("By 1351, between 30 and 60 percent of Europe was dead…")
- Mournful coda lines ("…and so the streets of Avignon fell silent.")

No questions to viewer. No second-person. No "imagine if you were there." The narrator is a memoirist, not a guide.

## [LOOP LOGIC]

Not a loop video — longform 12-min documentary structure. But the *channel* loops via "next episode tease" → adjacent event in same era/geography family ("Next: the Great Mortality reaches Russia, 1352"). Subscriber retention loops at the meta-channel level, not per-video.

If converted to Short (60s, 9:16): loop achieved by ending the Short on the cold-open of the next event ("…and 100 years later, in Constantinople…") with the next-video link in description.
